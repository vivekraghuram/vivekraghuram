Earth Structure & Plate Tectonics

The earth is composed of several layers of material. From the center outward are the inner core, outer core, lower mantle (Mesosphere), upper mantle (Asthenosphere) and the crust (Lithosphere). The core is mostly composed of iron (85%) while the crust is mostly oxygen (46%) and silicon (28%). The crust floats on top of the mantle.

The Continental Drift Theory states that the crust is broken into tectonic plates which move around as they float on the mantle. Their collisions and motion formed the continents and many geographic features like mountains and trenches. Some key evidence for this comes from the following sources:

  • Maps: The shapes of various landmasses appear to fit together
  • Fossil Record: Fossils of similar animals can be found across completely separate continents
  • Mid-Ocean Ridges: The are parallel lines of rock of increasing age around the ridge suggesting that the older rock moved away from the ridge
  • Patterns of Magnetism: Parallel lines of rock along ridges demonstrate have different polarities from Earth's polarity reversals
  • Earthquakes: The speed of seismic waves vary in earthquakes depending on location and depth

Navigation

Ye olde sailors would find land by looking for certain signs in their environment:

  • Clouds in the distance - as land heats up during the day, clouds form
  • Seabirds returning home at dusk
  • The presence of different kinds of sea life
  • Changes in wave patterns potentially deflecting off land
  • Changes in ocean temperature
  • Changes in color of water
  • Directions of the stars for general navigation

Thankfully sailors have more precise means for determining their location now. With the invention of an accurate clock, it became possible to exactly determine one's location using latitude and longitude.

OCEAN CARBON CHEMISTRY

The ocean dissolves large amounts of carbon in three different chemical compounds: carbonic acid (H2CO3), bicarbonate (HCO3-) and carbonate (CO32-). A large amount of this is from biogenous sediment allowing the ocean to absorb large amouts of atmospheric CO2. In fact, the amount of CO2 in the atmosphere is 30% lower thanks to the ocean's biological carbon pump.
